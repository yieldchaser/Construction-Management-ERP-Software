Tips for better Real-time Updates and Progress Tracking Conclusion FAQs What is Progress Tracking in Construction? On construction sites, there are multiple tasks assigned to subcontractors for example wall painting, wall building, balcony building, and so on. A contractor is responsible to take updates from subcontractors on those tasks and he makes sure that work is being done effectively. So, progress tracking is the process of evaluating and scrutinising the activities that are taking progress at a construction site so that the owner and contractor stay informed about the progress and take steps or control the activities accordingly. So, if you are someone who is looking for a perfect project management software tool then you can choose a software named ONSITE where you can do much more than just taking progress updates. Why is Real-time Progress Tracking important? Building a construction project is a teamwork and coordination is a necessary element in the teamwork. Using a project management software tool is one of the smartest steps to take towards better progress tracking. So, following are the reasons how it helps with productivity: 1. Effective Communication By getting software, you can track the progress updates within the app. Subcontractor can just provide updates in the app that will be notified to you through which you can just get the real-time update without having to make any call. This helps with enhancing the communication and removes any kind of misunderstanding. You can even ask for images of the site and see the progress by yourself. 2. Avoid Delays By efficient tracking and real-time updates, you can thoroughly analyse the progress speed and identify if there are any delays or issues. Through which you take actions to avoid those delays and motivate workers to grow productivity. You can set a timeline of a particular activity so that workers will work according to that timeline. o, through real-time progress tracking you can help the project to avoid delays. 3. Enhanced Decision Making Real-time data allows project managers to make decisions quickly according to the situation. If there are any issues at the site, the sooner you get that information the sooner you can make a decision to solve the issue. It helps to make your decision-making process enhanced and quick. 4. Performance Monitoring By tracking real-time updates, you can track the progress of workers on the site and see who is working efficiently. You can also give instructions if you see that the progress is slow or has completely stopped. You can also appreciate workers who are working efficiently on site. This performance assessment helps the workers stay aligned with goals and standards. 5. Quality Assurance Continuous tracking helps the workers to maintain high-quality standards. While a subcontractor shares a photo, a builder can immediately order changes and the immediate action can be taken. This helps with quality check and helps with avoiding any compromises with the project&#8217;s integrity. 6. Avoid Overbilling With real-time progress tracking, you can also track materials and avoid the overbilling to suppliers. Often the supplier supplies less amount of material and takes the full payment. By getting real-time updates you can avoid such situations. 7. Avoid Material Wastage By real-time updates, you can get to know how much material is needed at a site and by knowing the accurate material requirement you can avoid ordering extra material which helps in avoiding the material wastage. If the material wastage is controlled you are also getting benefits of cost saving. 8.
