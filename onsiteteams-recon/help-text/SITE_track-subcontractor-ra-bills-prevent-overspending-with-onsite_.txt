Without a system that respects project stages, teams often discover overspend only after cash flow is tight. How a Structured RA Bill Process Should Function The charging procedure for a reliable RA starts on site, not at the accounts desk. Before a bill is sent out, the work that has been done is measured where it took place and compared to the BOQ items. These checked amounts are what everyone uses as a starting point. The subcontractor then makes the RA bill utilizing those numbers that have been checked. Project teams check it against the agreed-upon pricing and scope, and any adjustments are clearly stated instead of being handled informally. After the bill is approved, it goes to the accounts department to be paid. There is a record of every version and approval for future reference. This billing procedure cuts down on disputes and speeds up payment cycles because everyone is working with the same information. Why Tracking RA Bills Matters? How Onsite Helps You Track RA Bills? 1.
