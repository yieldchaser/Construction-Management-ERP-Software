Construction Procurement as Collaboration, Not Just Oversight Onsite in Action Conclusion FAQs Why Construction Procurement Often Fails? In construction, procurement is about more than just making orders. It&#8217;s about filling site to office procurement gap. When requests go through a lot of different spreadsheets, emails, or messaging apps, they often cause problems. Orders sit unprocessed, deliveries are missed, and invoicing takes longer than they should. Work has slowed down and budgets have changed by the time approvals come through. The main cause of the problem is bad coordination.Â Procurement links the office to the site. If there isn&#8217;t a single system, the whole process falls apart, which causes delays and extra expenditures. How Onsite Streamlines the Construction Procurement Process? Onsite turns procurement into a seamless workflow: At the site: Supervisors can request materials directly from their mobile devices, including specifications, quantities, and photos. In the office: Procurement officers can approve, generate purchase orders, and track budget impacts in real time. With suppliers: Every order is tracked until delivery, with proof of arrival recorded digitally, reducing disputes. This visibility ensures everyone sees the same information, eliminating the delays caused by back-and-forth communication. FAQs Q1: What is construction procurement software? Construction procurement software helps manage the full process of sourcing, purchasing, and tracking materials and services needed for a project. It connects site teams, office staff, and suppliers in one digital system, reducing delays and errors. Q2: How does Onsite improve construction procurement? Onsite allows supervisors to request materials from mobile devices, lets office staff approve orders in real time, and tracks deliveries digitally. This ensures accurate, timely, and transparent procurement across all projects. Q3: Why do construction procurement processes often fail?
