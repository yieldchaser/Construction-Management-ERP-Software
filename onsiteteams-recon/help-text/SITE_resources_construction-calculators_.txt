Which IS code standards do the steel calculators follow? + All steel calculators use IS 456:2000 formulas. The unit weight formula is D&#178; &#247; 163 (kg/m), where D is bar diameter in mm. Lap lengths are calculated as 50D for columns. Stirrup cutting lengths account for 90&#176; and 135&#176; bend deductions as per IS standards. The Bar Bending Schedule calculator also follows SP 34 guidelines for shape codes. What is the difference between the one-way slab and two-way slab calculators? + A one-way slab (Ly/Lx greater than 2) carries load in the shorter direction only. Main bars run along the shorter span; distribution bars run in the longer direction. A two-way slab (Ly/Lx of 2 or less) carries load in both directions, so main reinforcement is needed along both Lx and Ly. Use the one-way calculator for beams spanning in one direction and the two-way for panels supported on all four sides. Does the brick calculator account for mortar and wastage? + Yes. The brick calculator adds a 10 mm mortar joint to both length and height when counting bricks. It also calculates wet and dry mortar volume separately, then breaks that down into cement bags and sand quantity using a 1:6 mix ratio. Wastage is adjustable â€” the default is 10%, which covers standard site cutting. Decorative or diagonal brickwork should use 15 to 20%.
