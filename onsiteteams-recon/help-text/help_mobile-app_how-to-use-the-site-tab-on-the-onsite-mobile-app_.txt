Before You Start You must be inside a project Opening the Site Tab Open a project and tap the Site tab. Top Bar The top of the Site tab shows three items: Element What It Shows Progress % Overall project completion percentage (e.g. 86%) Todo Count of pending To Do items assigned on this project. Tap to open them. Material Requests Shortcut to raise or view material requests. Tap to open the Material module. Use the left and right arrows (date navigator) to move between days and see the summary for any date. Day Summary Tiles The main section shows a snapshot of what was recorded on the selected day. Tile What It Shows Site Staff â€” Present Number of site staff marked present that day Equipment â€” Used Number of equipment usage entries logged Subcon â€” Updates Number of subcontractor progress updates recorded Material â€” Received Number of material receipt (GRN) entries for the day Material â€” Used Number of material consumption entries for the day A 0 on any tile means nothing was recorded for that category on that day. Use this as a quick daily check â€” if Equipment shows 0 and machines were on site, the equipment log may be missing. Tap the photo icon on any tile to add photos tagged to that category (e.g. tap the photo icon on Material Received to attach a delivery photo to that day&#8217;s GRN activity). Site Photos The Site Photos row shows a thumbnail strip of all photos uploaded for the selected day. Tap View All to see the full photo gallery for that day Tap the camera icon on the left to add a new photo for the selected date Photos uploaded here are tagged to the project and date and appear in the DPR&#8217;s Site Photos section. Ongoing Tasks The Ongoing Tasks section lists tasks currently in progress, showing the task name and current progress (e.g. &#8220;Fan Installation â€” 85 nos completed&#8221;). Tap View All to open the full task list for the project. Material Requests Tap the Material Requests button at the top of the Site tab to open the Material module. Four sub-tabs appear: Sub-tab What It Shows or Does Request Create a new material request. Select the date, tap + Add Material to add items, tap + Add Notes for context, then submit. Inventory View current stock levels for all materials on this project, grouped by category, with quantity on hand. Received View all GRN entries â€” materials received on site. Used View all material consumption entries â€” materials used from stock. The Material Requests shortcut on the Site tab means the supervisor does not need to navigate to a separate Material tab to raise a request â€” it is accessible directly from here. Creating and Downloading a DPR The DPR (Daily Progress Report) is a PDF summary of the project&#8217;s activity for a selected date. It compiles data already entered in the app â€” attendance, materials, equipment, subcontractor progress, and site photos â€” into a single shareable report. Steps From the Site tab, scroll to the bottom and tap Create DPR A section selector appears. All sections are checked by default. Uncheck any sections you do not want to include in this DPR Tap Download PDF A preview of the DPR opens â€” tap Download to save it to your device DPR Sections Section What It Contains Header Company name, site name, and date AI Recommendations Automatically generated insights flagged as High, Medium, or Low priority â€” covering task delays, material gaps, labour status, and subcon updates Work Summary Task list with today&#8217;s progress quantity and overall completion vs target Labour Report Party name, workforce category, number of workers, shift count, overtime hours Material Inventory Opening stock, received, used, and closing stock per material Material Requests Pending material requests with item names, quantities, and status Equipment Equipment name, number, total hours used, and fuel added Subcon Progress Summary Subcontractor work order titles and current progress Site Photos All photos uploaded for the day Sharing the DPR After downloading, tap the share icon to send the PDF directly to the project WhatsApp group or any other contact. Sharing the DPR with clients or management at the end of each working day keeps all stakeholders updated without requiring them to log into Onsite. Tips The day summary tiles are the fastest daily check. A 0 on any tile signals a missing entry. If Material Received shows 0 but materials arrived that day, a GRN has not been created. Use the date navigator to go back and review previous days. If a report is being prepared for yesterday or last week, navigate to the correct date before creating the DPR. Uncheck sections in the DPR selector when only specific data is needed. A client update DPR can include just Work Summary and Site Photos, skipping Labour and Material details. The AI Recommendations section in the DPR highlights critical items automatically â€” tasks falling behind schedule, inventory running low, or pending material requests. Review this section before the daily team briefing. The photo icon on each day summary tile lets you attach photos to a specific activity category. Tagging a photo to Material Received connects it to that day&#8217;s GRN activity for the DPR.
