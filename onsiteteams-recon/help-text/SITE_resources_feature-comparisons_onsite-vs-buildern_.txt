What is the main difference between Onsite and Buildern? + Buildern covers the full residential project lifecycle, from pre-sales CRM, estimating, and proposals through scheduling, subcontractor coordination, and client communication. Onsite focuses on BOQ-driven execution cost control: every site action links directly to budget positions in real time so financial movement is visible while the project is actively running. The core difference is lifecycle management versus execution cost control. Does Buildern support RA billing and BOQ-linked invoicing? + RA bill generation is not publicly highlighted in Buildern. Billing is progress and milestone-based. Onsite includes RA bill generation, BOQ-linked client invoicing, automatic retention deduction, and collection tracking as built-in modules designed for Indian and Gulf construction billing workflows. Can Onsite replace Buildern for pre-sales and estimating? + Onsite includes lead tracking, CRM, BOQ-based estimating, and proposal generation. However, Buildern&#8217;s depth in quantity takeoff, branded proposals, cost catalogs with supplier pricing, and pre-construction workflow is stronger for residential contractors whose primary challenge is winning work and managing client relationships before a project starts. For contractors who have already won projects and need execution cost control, Onsite is the stronger fit. Is Onsite suitable for residential builders? + Yes, particularly residential builders and developers in India and Gulf who need BOQ-linked cost control, labour management with geo-tagged attendance, material tracking, subcontractor billing, and RA bill generation during project execution. Buildern may be better suited for residential contractors in North American markets who prioritise pre-sales workflows, client portals, and QuickBooks or Xero integration. How does Onsite handle labour management compared to Buildern? + Onsite includes a dedicated labour management module with geo-tagged attendance, face recognition, automated salary and wages, real-time labour cost versus budget tracking, and subcontractor labour separation. Buildern does not have a dedicated labour management module. Timesheet tracking is available via scheduling, but geo-tagged attendance and face recognition are not available in Buildern. Does Buildern work for construction teams in India? + Buildern can be used in India for project scheduling, budgeting, and client communication. However, it integrates with QuickBooks and Xero rather than Tally or Zoho Books, does not include RA bill generation, and operates on USD pricing. Indian teams that need BOQ-linked cost control, Tally integration, geo-tagged labour attendance, and local billing compliance would require additional tools alongside Buildern. Which platform is better for multi-level approval workflows? + Onsite. Multi-level approval workflows are a core Onsite module covering material requests, purchase orders, vendor bills, subcontractor payments, and expense releases, each with configurable approval chains and a full documented audit trail. Buildern does not offer multi-level approval workflows for procurement or billing. Should I choose Onsite or Buildern for my construction business? + Choose Buildern if your priority is managing the full project lifecycle from pre-sales through handover, particularly in a residential or custom home context with strong estimating, proposal generation, and QuickBooks or Xero integration. Choose Onsite if your priority is BOQ-driven cost control, RA billing, multi-level approvals, labour management, and a complete execution-to-finance workflow from one INR-priced platform built for India and Gulf construction markets.
