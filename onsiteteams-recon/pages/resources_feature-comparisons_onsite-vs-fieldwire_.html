Platform Overview

Two Different Approaches to Construction Software

Fieldwire is built for drawing management and field coordination. Onsite is built around BOQ-linked execution where every field update drives a financial outcome.

● Onsite

What is Onsite

Onsite is a construction management software built specifically around BOQ-driven execution, cost control, and live site visibility. It connects scope definition, installed quantities, labour attendance, material usage, subcontractor billing, and project finances into one continuous workflow. Every field action feeds directly into budget position and billing readiness.

Onsite is best suited for construction companies that want:
  • ✓BOQ-linked budgeting, progress tracking, and billing in one workflow
  • ✓Real-time capture of labour, material, and site activity tied to cost
  • ✓Integrated subcontractor work orders, running bills, and retention tracking
  • ✓Budget versus actual dashboards updated by execution, not manual entry
  • ✓Native integration with Tally and Zoho Books for accounting sync
Why construction teams choose Onsite:
  • ✓Designed for execution-centric cost control — not drawing governance or documentation management
  • ✓Works best for contractors managing multiple active projects with BOQ-based scope
  • ✓Built specifically for India and Gulf construction workflows with INR pricing and RA billing
  • ✓Focuses on live commercial clarity rather than post-execution reporting assembled from separate tools

Pricing: Onsite follows a project-friendly pricing model designed for construction teams of different sizes, with INR-based tiers and dedicated onboarding support.

● Fieldwire

What is Fieldwire

Fieldwire is a construction project management software built for drawing management and field coordination. It connects site teams and office teams around a single current drawing set, replacing scattered plan distribution and communication with a centralised platform for markups, RFIs, submittals, punchlists, task assignments, and progress documentation.

Fieldwire is best suited for construction companies that want:
  • ✓Plan viewing with automatic version control so every team member works from current drawings
  • ✓RFI and submittal management with structured approval and response tracking
  • ✓Punchlist and issue tracking linked directly to specific drawing locations
  • ✓Task assignment tied to drawings and accessible offline on mobile for field teams
  • ✓Multi-language support for globally distributed teams across North America, Europe, and international markets
Why construction teams choose Fieldwire:
  • ✓Designed primarily for drawing management and field documentation — not BOQ-linked cost control
  • ✓Works best for general contractors and subcontractors where coordination around current drawings is the core need
  • ✓Offers a free plan for small teams wanting to evaluate the platform before committing to a paid tier
  • ✓Supports 2D and 3D plan access, BIM viewing, and Box, Dropbox, and OneDrive document sync

Pricing: Fieldwire offers a free plan with limited features. Paid plans are priced in USD and include web access, BIM viewing, custom report export, and change order management at the Business Plus tier.

Key Differentiators

Key Differences That Matter in Practice: Onsite vs Fieldwire

Four operational areas where the platforms diverge — each with direct impact on cost visibility, labour accuracy, and execution control on your live projects.

Drawing Management vs Cost Control
Comparison
✓ Onsite

Onsite connects every field action directly to a financial outcome. Installed quantities, labour attendance entries, and material consumption do not exist as separate activity records — each one updates budget position, cost variance, and billing readiness in real time. A site engineer recording progress in the morning affects the project P&L the same day. Financial clarity is the product of execution, not a separate reporting exercise.

● Fieldwire

Fieldwire is one of the strongest platforms available for drawing management and field coordination. Automatic version control ensures every team member works from the current drawing set. Markups, RFIs, submittals, and punchlist management are built with the field team as the primary user. Its positioning centres on coordination accuracy and documentation quality rather than continuous commercial recalculation.

✓ Onsite

Onsite embeds financial discipline into day-to-day execution workflows. Material requests, procurement decisions, and expense approvals are checked against BOQ budget limits through multi-level approval chains before any commitment is made. This means cost overruns are flagged at the point of decision — not discovered during a period-end review when it is too late to act. The platform alerts you to a loss while the project is still running.

● Fieldwire

Fieldwire provides budget monitoring at the Business Plus tier and supports cost reporting across projects. Its emphasis, however, is on field coordination transparency rather than preventive financial gating tied strictly to BOQ quantities. Budget controls in Fieldwire depend more on user discipline at the reporting stage than on structural enforcement at the commitment stage.

✓ Onsite

Onsite provides geo-tagged attendance with face-based validation options, making proxy attendance and ghost worker entries structurally difficult. Confirmed attendance links directly to payroll calculations, closing the loop between site presence and wage cost. Subcontractor labour is tracked separately, giving project managers visibility into workforce composition and productivity across all active sites.

● Fieldwire

Fieldwire does not offer a dedicated labour management module. Crew hours can be recorded through forms and time sheets, but there is no geo-tagged attendance, no face recognition validation, no automated payroll calculation from attendance data, and no subcontractor labour separation as a structured workflow within the platform.

✓ Onsite

Onsite is built specifically for India and Gulf construction workflows. INR-based pricing, RA bill generation linked to BOQ progress, Tally and Zoho Books native integration, and retention management are all part of the platform — not workarounds assembled from separate tools. For contractors operating under Indian construction contract norms, these are not nice-to-have additions but daily operational requirements.

● Fieldwire

Fieldwire is a globally deployed platform supporting over twenty languages and operating across projects in North America, Europe, and internationally. Its pricing is in USD and its financial tools do not include RA bill generation, BOQ-linked invoicing, or Tally and Zoho Books integration. Teams requiring India-specific billing workflows or Gulf-market accounting integration would need to operate Fieldwire alongside separate financial tools.

FAQ

Questions About Onsite vs Fieldwire

Common questions when comparing both platforms for Indian and Gulf construction teams.

Fieldwire is built for drawing management and field coordination. Automatic version control ensures every team member works from the current drawing set, and markups, RFIs, submittals, punchlists, and task assignments all operate within that drawing-centred structure. The platform eliminates miscommunication caused by version confusion and scattered documentation across field and office teams. Onsite treats every field action as a structured financial input. Installed quantities, labour attendance, material consumption, and procurement decisions all connect directly to BOQ budget positions and billing readiness in real time. Financial clarity is the product of execution itself — not a separate reporting exercise assembled at period end from disconnected data sources.

Fieldwire provides budget monitoring at its Business Plus plan tier and supports cost reporting across projects. However, its positioning does not centre the BOQ as the primary structural control layer for all workflows. Budget tracking in Fieldwire depends more on how consistently users maintain data entry discipline than on structural platform linkage that enforces alignment automatically. Onsite builds the BOQ as the operational backbone of execution — budgets, procurement, progress updates, billing, and margin analysis all trace back to defined BOQ line items without requiring manual discipline to keep them aligned. Purchasing decisions are checked against BOQ budget limits through multi-level approval workflows before any commitment is made, making cost overrun prevention structural rather than procedural.

Onsite provides a dedicated labour management module with geo-tagged attendance, face-based validation to prevent proxy entries, direct payroll calculation from confirmed attendance data, subcontractor labour separation, and real-time labour cost allocation against project budgets. For construction workforces where payroll accuracy is tied directly to site presence, this chain closes the gap between who was on site and what was paid. Fieldwire does not offer a dedicated labour management module. Crew hours can be recorded through time sheets and forms, but there is no geo-tagged attendance, no face recognition validation, no automated payroll calculation from attendance records, and no subcontractor labour separation as a structured workflow within the platform. Teams using Fieldwire typically handle labour tracking and payroll through separate systems.

Onsite provides structured subcontractor management that includes digital work orders linked to BOQ scope, progress-based milestone billing, built-in retention tracking, running bill generation, and subcontractor productivity analytics. For contractors managing multiple subcontractors on BOQ-based contracts, these features prevent payment disputes and ensure retention releases are tied to verified completion rather than manual agreement. Fieldwire supports subcontractor access to the platform and allows subcontractors to participate in task and punchlist workflows alongside the general contractor. However, digital work orders, running bill generation, retention management, and structured approval workflows for subcontractor billing are not part of Fieldwire’s feature set. Teams needing structured financial management of subcontractor relationships would require separate tools alongside Fieldwire.

Onsite covers drawing and document management — plan viewing, markups, RFIs, submittals, change orders, version control, and offline access to plans are all part of the platform. These features support execution workflows rather than functioning as a standalone drawing management system. Fieldwire genuinely excels at drawing management and field coordination in a way that reflects years of product focus on that specific problem. Automatic version sync, BIM access, deep markup tooling, and structured RFI and submittal workflows are core strengths. For teams where drawing accuracy and documentation governance are the primary operational challenge — particularly globally distributed teams or those with complex documentation requirements — Fieldwire’s depth in this area is meaningful. Onsite is the stronger choice when cost control and financial visibility during execution are the primary requirements, with drawing management as one component of a broader workflow.

Onsite links every material request to a specific BOQ budget line before it enters the procurement workflow. Multi-level approval chains check budget availability at the moment a request is raised — not at the reporting stage. The complete procurement chain covers RFQ, quotation comparison, PO generation, GRN recording, delivery tracking, and automated three-way bill matching between purchase order, goods receipt, and vendor invoice. Every purchase is structurally linked to the project budget. Fieldwire does not include a procurement or material management module. There is no RFQ workflow, no purchase order generation, no GRN recording, no three-way bill matching, and no site inventory tracking within the platform. Teams using Fieldwire handle procurement and material management entirely through separate systems, which means cost data from procurement decisions is not structurally connected to progress data on site.

Fieldwire is a globally deployed platform and is technically accessible in India. However, its pricing is in USD and its financial tools do not include RA bill generation, BOQ-linked invoicing, or native integration with Tally and Zoho Books — the accounting systems most commonly used by Indian construction companies. Teams operating under Indian construction contract norms, where RA billing and retention management are standard requirements, would need to run separate financial tools alongside Fieldwire to cover these workflows. Onsite is built specifically for India and Gulf construction workflows. Pricing is INR-based, RA bill generation linked to BOQ progress is a core platform feature, and Tally and Zoho Books integration is available natively. The relevant comparison is not cost alone but what each platform delivers for India-specific operational requirements out of the box versus through separate tools.

Choose Fieldwire if drawing management and field coordination are the core problem you need to solve — particularly if your teams are spread across multiple locations, version confusion on drawings is causing rework, or RFI and submittal tracking is a daily operational requirement. Fieldwire is also the right choice if you want a free entry-level plan, need BIM access, or are operating across international markets where multi-language support matters. Choose Onsite if your projects run on BOQ-based contracts where daily site activity must directly drive cost visibility, billing readiness, and margin control — and where the gap between what happens on site and what the finance team sees is costing money. Onsite is the right fit for contractors, builders, EPC firms, and subcontractors in India and the Gulf who need execution, labour, procurement, subcontractor management, and billing to run from one platform without stitching separate tools together.
