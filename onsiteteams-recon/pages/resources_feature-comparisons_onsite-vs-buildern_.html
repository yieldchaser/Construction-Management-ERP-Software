Platform Overview

Two Different Approaches to Construction Software

Both Onsite and Buildern operate on active construction projects , but they are built for fundamentally different priorities. Understanding their approach helps you choose the right fit for your team.

● Onsite

What is Onsite

Onsite is a construction management software built around BOQ-driven execution and cost control. Every site action , material requests, labour entries, progress updates, and billing approvals , links directly to budget positions in real time, so financial movement is visible while the project is actively running. Built specifically for contractors, developers, and EPC firms across India and the Gulf.

Onsite is best suited for construction companies that want:
  • ✓BOQ-linked budgeting, progress tracking, and client billing from one platform
  • ✓Real-time capture of labour, material, and site activity during execution
  • ✓Integrated subcontractor work orders, running bills, and retention management
  • ✓Multi-level approval workflows before procurement or expenses are committed
  • ✓Native Tally and Zoho Books integration with no double entry between site and finance
Why construction teams choose Onsite:
  • ✓Execution-centered cost control that connects scope, billing, and budget as the project progresses
  • ✓Works best for BOQ-based contractors in India and Gulf managing concurrent active projects
  • ✓Live commercial clarity during execution rather than assembled at period end

Pricing: INR-based per user per year , designed for Indian and Gulf construction teams of all sizes, not scaled to construction volume.

● Buildern

What is Buildern

Buildern is a construction management platform built around the full residential project lifecycle. Lead tracking, CRM, estimating, quantity takeoff, cost catalog with supplier pricing, branded proposal generation, scheduling, subcontractor coordination, and a client communication portal all operate within one system , giving residential builders and custom home contractors a complete administrative and operational workflow from the first client conversation through project handover.

Buildern is typically suited for teams that need:
  • ✓Full pre-sales workflow , lead tracking, bid management, quantity takeoff, and branded proposals in one system
  • ✓Cost catalog management with supplier price integration for accurate residential project estimating
  • ✓Client communication portal with free access for clients, vendors, and subcontractors
  • ✓Integration with QuickBooks or Xero as the primary accounting platform
Important considerations:
  • ⚠No BOQ-linked execution cost control , budget vs actual not driven by live site activity
  • ⚠No dedicated labour management module , geo-tagged attendance and face recognition not available
  • ⚠No multi-level approval workflows for procurement or vendor payments
  • ⚠No RA bill generation and no native Tally or Zoho Books integration
  • ⚠USD pricing , not built for India or Gulf construction workflows and compliance

Pricing: USD-based pricing designed for residential contractors in North American and international English-speaking markets. Indian and Gulf teams would require separate tools for RA billing, labour management, and Tally or Zoho Books integration.

Key Differentiators

Key Differences Between Onsite and Buildern That Impact Daily Project Control

Three critical areas where Onsite and Buildern take fundamentally different approaches, each with real impact on how your team manages active projects.

Residential Lifecycle vs Execution Cost Control
Comparison
✓ Onsite

Onsite places clear focus on connecting site activity to financial outcomes. Labour entries, procurement approvals, material deliveries, and progress updates feed directly into cost tracking and budget positions as they occur. The financial picture available to project managers reflects real site activity in real time rather than being assembled from separate data sources at period end. For contractors managing multiple concurrent projects with BOQ-driven scope, this live cost connection is the core value.

● Buildern

Buildern covers the complete project lifecycle for residential and custom home contractors, from the first client lead through estimating, proposals, scheduling, subcontractor coordination, and client communication. The platform gives smaller construction businesses a single system covering both administrative and operational sides of running a project. The estimating, cost catalog, and client proposal features are particular strengths for builders managing individual home projects.

✓ Onsite

Onsite concentrates on financial discipline during day-to-day execution. Approvals and workflows are aligned with budget limits before expenses are committed, keeping control at the execution stage rather than only at the reporting stage. The distinction matters most for contractors who need to see the cost impact of execution decisions before those decisions are finalized, not after funds have already been spent.

● Buildern

Buildern’s strongest differentiator is its pre-sales and estimating capability. Lead tracking, bid management, quantity takeoff, cost catalogs with supplier price management, and professional proposal generation give residential builders a comprehensive pre-construction workflow. For contractors whose primary challenge is winning work and managing client relationships through the pre-construction phase, Buildern addresses this directly with depth that most construction ERP platforms do not offer.

✓ Onsite

Onsite is built specifically for India and Gulf construction workflows. INR-based pricing, RA bill generation, Tally and Zoho Books native integration, BOQ-linked progress and billing, retention management, and geo-tagged labour attendance are all part of the platform rather than requiring additional tools alongside it. Indian and Gulf contractors get a complete execution and financial system without stitching together multiple products.

● Buildern

Buildern integrates with QuickBooks and Xero, operates on USD pricing, and is designed primarily for residential contractors in North American and English-speaking markets. Its billing workflows do not include RA bill generation, and it does not integrate natively with Tally or Zoho Books. Indian and Gulf construction companies using Buildern would require separate tools for RA billing, labour management, and local accounting integration.
