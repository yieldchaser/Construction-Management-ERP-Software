How Onsite Helps Interior Companies Run Tighter Projects
Interior work is fast, messy, and client-facing. The margin for error is thin. Here is what changes when you move from spreadsheets and WhatsApp to Onsite.
Before Vendor bills arrive and get paid. Nobody checks whether the work was actually done or matches the purchase order. Disputes happen later when it’s too late to recover the money.
With Onsite Every vendor bill is matched to a purchase order and delivery record before it can be approved. Multi-level sign-offs mean nothing gets paid without the right person seeing it first.
Before Scope grows through WhatsApp messages and verbal agreements. By the time you calculate the actual project cost, you’re already over budget with no documentation to back a change order.

Before Labour attendance is marked manually by a supervisor. Payroll is calculated at month end from a register that may or may not reflect what actually happened on site.
With Onsite GPS-tagged attendance and face recognition replace the register entirely. Salary calculations run from verified attendance data — no manual reconciliation, no disputes.

Interior project management software is a platform that helps interior design firms manage their projects from quotation to client handover in one place. It covers scope tracking, vendor billing, material management, labour attendance, and project profit visibility. Interior designers need it because managing three or more projects simultaneously using WhatsApp, Excel, and phone calls leads to billing disputes, cost overruns, and missed deadlines that are only discovered after the project ends.
Onsite is purpose-built for construction and interior companies in India and the Middle East. Unlike generic project management tools such as Asana, Monday, or Notion, Onsite includes construction-specific modules: GPS attendance with face recognition, material tracking with PO matching, vendor billing approval workflows, project P&L linked to daily site activity, and Tally integration.
Generic tools are built for software teams, marketing agencies, and office-based workflows. They do not handle purchase orders, vendor bills, GPS attendance, material stock levels, or construction-specific billing like retention and RA bills. Onsite is built specifically for site-based businesses where the work happens on location, not in a browser.
Accurate cost tracking for interior projects requires three things working together: every vendor bill matched to a purchase order before payment, material usage logged against a project budget rather than a shared sheet, and labour costs calculated from verified attendance rather than supervisor estimates. Onsite connects all three into a single system so the project P&L updates in real time.
