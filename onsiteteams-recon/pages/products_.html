Where Construction Money Leaks and How Onsite Stops It

Most construction businesses lose money not on big decisions but on small daily gaps — untracked materials, unverified attendance, delayed billing, and idle equipment. Onsite closes every one of these gaps before they become losses.

Stop Material Wastage at the Source

Most sites lose lakhs every year to over-ordering, pilferage, and untracked consumption. Onsite gives your team budget-linked material requests, GRN-based delivery tracking, and real-time stock visibility — so every kilogram of material is accounted for from purchase to usage. You know exactly what arrived, what was used, and what is missing before the month ends.

Make Every Labour Hour Count

When attendance runs on paper registers and productivity goes unmeasured, you pay for work that never happened. Onsite captures geo-tagged attendance with face recognition, tracks workforce output activity by activity, and calculates wages only against verified data — so your payroll reflects actual work, and your site team stays accountable every single day.

Bill Faster and Collect Without Disputes

Delayed invoices and manual billing errors choke your cash flow and invite client disputes. Onsite generates invoices directly from your BOQ and progress data, so every bill reflects only completed work. You track what is billed, what is collected, and what is pending — all in one dashboard — and your finance team stops chasing approvals across WhatsApp.

Track Every Asset Before It Goes Missing

Equipment sitting idle or tools lost between sites drain money silently. Onsite assigns every asset to a project or team, tracks operating hours and location, and logs maintenance and repair costs — so you always know where your equipment is, how it is being used, and when it needs attention before a breakdown stalls your site.

FAQs

What plans does Onsite offer and what does each include?

Onsite offers three plans built for different stages of a construction business. The Business Plan at Rs 12,000 per user per year covers core execution modules including tasks, progress tracking, labour and materials. The Business Plus Plan at Rs 15,000 per user per year adds advanced BOQ, warehouse management, procurement and payroll. The Enterprise Plan starts at Rs 10 lakh and includes unlimited users, custom integrations and white-label options. All plans require a minimum of three users.

Which modules does Onsite cover and do I need to use all of them?

Onsite covers every stage of construction execution including sales and tendering, design management, planning and budgeting, progress tracking, material and inventory management, labour and salaries, subcontractor management, assets and equipment, procurement, BOQ and billing, quality management, finance and accounts, and team controls. You do not need to activate every module on day one. Most teams start with two or three modules that solve their biggest pain points and expand from there as adoption grows across the business.

How long does it take to get started on Onsite?

Most construction companies go live on Onsite within 48 hours of onboarding. The setup process covers project creation, team access, role assignments and module configuration. Onsite also offers a Managed Service for Rs 15,000 per project per month where a dedicated coordinator handles daily supervisor calls, WhatsApp photo audits and progress reports, making adoption faster even when your site team resists change. Enterprise clients receive custom implementation support based on the scale of their operations.

Can site engineers and supervisors use Onsite directly from the field?

Yes. Onsite is built mobile-first, meaning every core function works from a smartphone on site. Site engineers update daily progress reports, capture geo-tagged photos with timestamps, mark attendance, raise material requests and flag delays all from the field without needing to return to the office. This eliminates the dependency on WhatsApp for site updates and gives project managers and owners real-time visibility without waiting for end-of-day calls or Excel summaries.

Does Onsite work for construction companies in the Middle East as well as India?

Onsite serves construction companies across ten plus countries including India and the Middle East. The platform supports the workflows used by contractors, interior fit-out firms, MEP companies and EPC businesses in both markets. Clients in Doha, Dubai, Riyadh and across the Gulf use Onsite for subcontractor management, progress tracking and finance controls. The system handles multi-currency operations and adapts to the approval hierarchies and billing structures common in Middle East construction projects.

How does Onsite handle data security and role-based access?

Onsite gives every company full control over who sees what. Administrators assign roles including site engineer, project manager, accountant and procurement team, and each role carries specific permissions that restrict access to sensitive financial or project data. When an employee leaves or a device is lost, data stays protected within the platform. Every action taken by every user is logged with a timestamp, creating a complete audit trail that protects the business and eliminates data disputes between teams.

Is Onsite suitable for companies currently managing everything on Excel and WhatsApp?

Onsite is built specifically for construction companies moving away from Excel and WhatsApp. These tools create data silos, delay decisions and offer zero accountability. Your project manager updates a sheet, your site engineer sends photos on WhatsApp, and your owner still has no real picture of what is happening on site. Onsite replaces all of this with one connected system where tasks, materials, attendance, approvals and reports flow together automatically, giving every stakeholder the information they need without chasing anyone for updates.

Onsite is a construction management software built to help construction companies run projects with clarity and control. As a complete construction ERP software, it connects site teams, project managers, procurement, and finance on one platform. By replacing scattered tools and manual tracking, Onsite construction software helps businesses save time, reduce cost leakages, and improve execution across every project. The result is a practical ERP software that supports real site operations, not just reports.
