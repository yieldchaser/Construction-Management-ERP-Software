FAQs

What is construction CRM software?

Construction CRM software is a platform built specifically for contractors, builders, and developers to manage the pre-sales cycle from first enquiry to confirmed project. Unlike generic CRM tools designed for retail or service businesses, construction CRM handles BOQ-linked quotations, rate libraries with margin analysis, multi-channel lead capture from Facebook and WhatsApp, and direct conversion of confirmed leads into active projects carrying agreed scope and rates into the execution system without manual re-entry.

How does Onsite construction CRM work?

Onsite CRM works across four connected steps. Leads enter automatically from Facebook ads, WhatsApp messages, website forms, and direct referrals into one structured pipeline with source tracking and conversion stage visibility. Sales teams generate BOQ-linked quotations in minutes from a verified rate library with cost breakdown and margin analysis built in. Automated follow-up reminders ensure every active lead gets contacted on schedule. When a lead is confirmed, it converts directly into an active project carrying the agreed BOQ and scope into execution without manual handover.

How is Onsite CRM different from generic CRM tools like Zoho or Salesforce?

Generic CRM tools manage contacts, deals, and pipelines but have no construction-specific capabilities. They cannot generate BOQ-linked quotations, maintain a construction rate library with margin analysis, or convert a confirmed lead directly into a project with BOQ and scope carried into execution. Onsite is built for construction workflows specifically, connecting the pre-sales cycle to project planning, budgeting, and execution in one platform. A contractor using Zoho for CRM still needs separate tools for quotations, BOQs, and project management. Onsite replaces all three.

How does Onsite prevent margin erosion in construction quotations?

Margin erosion in construction quotations happens when rates are copied from old project BOQs without verification against current procurement costs. Onsite prevents this through a centralized rate library that sales teams maintain and update regularly, accessible to every team member generating quotations. Cost breakdown and profit margin analysis are visible before any quote leaves the system. Sales teams see exactly what margin a project will deliver before committing to a price rather than discovering the margin was insufficient when procurement costs arrive weeks later.

How does Onsite handle leads from multiple sources like Facebook and WhatsApp?

Onsite imports leads automatically from Facebook ad campaigns, website contact forms, and WhatsApp without requiring manual entry. Every lead that enters the system carries its source tag, assigned owner, project type, and initial status. Sales managers see total pipeline volume broken down by source, conversion stage, and probability from one dashboard without asking team members for manual updates. Lead source analytics show which channels produce the most profitable projects, enabling data-driven decisions about where to invest sales and marketing effort.

How do automated follow-up reminders work in Onsite CRM?

Every active lead in Onsite carries a follow-up date and an assigned owner. When a follow-up date arrives, Onsite sends an automatic reminder to the responsible sales team member through the app. The reminder shows the lead details, last conversation summary, and current deal stage so the team member has full context before making contact. Follow-up history gets logged automatically after each interaction. Sales managers see which leads have pending follow-ups, which are overdue, and which team members are consistently following through on their pipeline.

How does Onsite convert a confirmed lead into a project?

When a lead reaches confirmed status in Onsite, the system converts the enquiry into an active project in one action. The agreed BOQ, scope details, client information, and rate commitments made during the sales process carry directly into the project execution system without manual re-entry or verbal handover to the site team. Project managers and site engineers start with complete, verified information from day one. The numbers that won the deal are the same numbers the execution team works from, eliminating the scope mismatches and margin surprises that verbal handovers create.

Can Onsite CRM handle quotation revisions and version control?

Yes. Every revision to a quotation in Onsite gets saved as a separate version with the date, rate changes, and scope modifications recorded. Sales teams compare versions before sending the final quote to ensure the most accurate rates and margins are reflected. When clients request revisions, the original version remains accessible for reference. This eliminates the version confusion that happens when quotations get revised multiple times across email threads and WhatsApp conversations, where nobody can confirm with certainty which version a client accepted.
